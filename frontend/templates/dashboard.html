Learn About Insider Threats

Understand the psychology, indicators, and mechanics behind insider risks.

Who is an Insider?

Employees, contractors, or vendors. Harm can be accidental (negligence) or intentional (malicious).

What Makes a Threat?

Motivations: financial gain, revenge, coercion. Risk increases where access is broad and oversight is weak.

Indicators

Red flags: odd working hours, sudden large downloads, repeated login failures, or sudden behavioral changes.

Preventive Measures

Least privilege, MFA, continuous monitoring, behavioral analytics, and audits.

Detection Heuristics

This demo uses time anomalies (after-hours), login spikes, large egress events, and authorization violations.

Case Snippet

A finance user downloads salary files late at night and uploads them to personal cloud -> Alert Triggered.
